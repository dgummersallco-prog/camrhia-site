Gallery delivery time is one of the most common sources of post-wedding anxiety for couples — and one of the more manageable expectation-setting problems for photographers.

## What's typical

Industry turnaround times vary widely, but a common range is **4 to 8 weeks** for the full edited gallery, with some photographers delivering a small sneak-peek gallery (10-30 images) within the first week. Full turnaround beyond 8 weeks isn't unusual either, particularly during peak wedding season when a photographer may be editing multiple weddings' worth of images at once.

## Why it takes as long as it does

A full wedding gallery often includes 600-1,000+ final images, each individually culled and edited from a much larger raw shoot — sometimes 3,000+ frames. This isn't a quick batch-process; consistent color grading, exposure correction, and selection across an entire wedding day takes real, unhurried time to do well.

## For photographers: the value of just saying the number

Vague turnaround language ("you'll have your photos soon!") tends to generate more anxious check-in messages than a specific stated timeline, even a longer one. Couples generally handle "8 weeks" far better than an open-ended "soon" — specificity reduces anxiety even when the number itself isn't small.

## For couples: what's reasonable to ask

If you're well past your photographer's stated turnaround time with no update, a polite check-in is completely reasonable — most delays are logistical, not a sign anything's wrong. If a photographer goes significantly past their own stated timeline with no communication at all, that's a fair thing to follow up on directly.

## A sneak peek helps both sides

Many photographers now deliver a small preview gallery within the first week specifically to manage this anxiety — it doesn't shorten the real turnaround, but it meaningfully reduces the emotional wait, since the couple gets *something* tangible early.
