Photography tends to book out further in advance than most other wedding vendors, which surprises a lot of couples early in planning.

## The general guideline

**12 to 18 months** before the wedding is a common recommendation, especially for popular photographers or peak wedding season (typically late spring through early fall in most regions). Popular Saturdays in desirable markets can book out even earlier for in-demand photographers.

## Why photography specifically books up faster

Unlike a caterer or a rental company, who can often serve multiple events in the same general timeframe, a photographer can only physically shoot one wedding on a given date. That scarcity — combined with the fact that photography is one of the more emotionally important vendor decisions for most couples — means desirable photographers get locked in earlier than almost anyone else in the vendor lineup.

## If you're getting a later start

It's not a dead end — photographers do have last-minute cancellations, and some deliberately keep a portion of their calendar open for shorter-notice bookings. If your timeline is tighter than ideal:

- Be flexible on your exact date or day of the week if at all possible
- Reach out to multiple photographers at once rather than one at a time
- Ask directly whether they have any last-minute openings, rather than assuming a "no" from their general availability

## A practical order of operations

Because of how far out photography typically needs to be locked in, many wedding planners recommend booking your venue and photographer close together, before finalizing many of the smaller vendor decisions — since your date and location often need to be locked before you can seriously start photographer outreach anyway.
