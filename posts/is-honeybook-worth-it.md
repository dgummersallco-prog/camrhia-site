HoneyBook is one of the most widely used client-management platforms across creative industries, and it's earned that position honestly — it's a genuinely capable, well-built tool. Here's a fair, balanced look at where it fits well for wedding photographers, and where it doesn't.

## What HoneyBook does well

- **General client management**: contracts, invoicing, and scheduling are all solid, mature features
- **Cross-industry flexibility**: because it's built for creative businesses broadly, not just wedding photography, it works reasonably well if you also take on non-wedding client work
- **Established ecosystem**: a long track record, extensive documentation, and a large user base means most questions already have an answer somewhere

## Where it's a genuinely reasonable choice

If your business isn't exclusively wedding photography — if you also shoot portraits, brand sessions, or other client work — HoneyBook's general-purpose design is an asset, not a limitation, since it doesn't assume any one type of project.

## Where it falls short for wedding-specific work

HoneyBook's client portal lets a couple *view* information — contracts, invoices, project updates — but it wasn't built around wedding-specific collaborative planning. There's no native concept of a shared, jointly-editable day-of timeline, which is arguably the single most collaborative document in the entire wedding planning process. The couple's experience is fundamentally that of a client checking a portal, not a co-planner building something alongside their photographer.

## The honest verdict

HoneyBook is a strong, defensible choice if you want an established, general-purpose tool and your photographer-facing workflow (contracts, invoicing, scheduling) is your main priority. It's a weaker fit if the actual problem you're trying to solve is keeping a couple genuinely, actively in sync with you throughout planning — that's a narrower, more specific need that general-purpose tools tend not to address particularly well.
