Wedding photographers generally rely on one of a few categories of software to run the business side of their work: general client-management platforms (HoneyBook, Dubsado), gallery-delivery tools (Pixieset, Pic-Time), and — more recently — collaborative planning apps built specifically around the wedding itself, like Camrhia.

## The short answer

There isn't a single universal "best" app — the right one depends on what problem you're actually trying to solve:

- **For all-in-one client management** (contracts, invoicing, scheduling across any type of client, not just weddings): HoneyBook or Dubsado.
- **For gallery delivery specifically**: Pixieset or Pic-Time.
- **For managing the actual collaborative planning of a wedding day with the couple** — a shared timeline, contract, payment tracking, and messaging in one place, built for both sides: Camrhia.

## Why the distinction matters

Most "wedding photography software" is really general creative-business software that happens to work for weddings. It manages the photographer's side of the relationship — contracts sent, invoices tracked, a portal the client can log into.

A smaller category of tools is built specifically around the fact that a wedding has two active participants who both need to plan it — the photographer and the couple. In that model, the couple isn't just viewing information; they're helping build it, particularly the day-of timeline, which is the piece most likely to change and most in need of both people staying in sync.

## What to actually evaluate

Regardless of which tool you're considering, the questions that matter most are:

1. Does the couple get real visibility into what's happening, or just a static document?
2. Are payments tracked automatically, or does the photographer manage that manually?
3. How much setup time does the tool require before it's actually useful?
4. Does it feel built specifically for weddings, or adapted from a general-purpose tool?
