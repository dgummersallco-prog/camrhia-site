If you've spent any time searching for wedding photography business software, you've run into the same three names over and over: HoneyBook, Dubsado, and — if you're reading this — Camrhia. They solve overlapping problems, but they're not the same tool, and picking the wrong one usually means switching again in a year.

Here's an honest breakdown, without the marketing gloss.

## HoneyBook

HoneyBook is the most established name in this space, and for good reason — it's a genuinely capable all-purpose client management tool used across many creative industries, not just photography. Contracts, invoices, and a client portal are all solid.

Where it shows its age for *wedding-specific* work: it wasn't built around a wedding day's actual structure. There's no native concept of a shared, collaborative timeline — the thing that actually needs to get built *with* a couple, not just sent *to* them. Couples get a portal to view things, but not really a workspace to build the day alongside their photographer.

## Dubsado

Dubsado is the power-user's choice — deeply customizable workflows, forms, and automation. If you want to build an intricate, fully bespoke client journey from first inquiry to final delivery, Dubsado gives you the most raw flexibility of the three.

The tradeoff is setup time. Dubsado's flexibility comes with a real learning curve, and most photographers spend weeks (some spend months) building out their workflows before the tool actually saves them time instead of costing it. It's also, like HoneyBook, fundamentally a *photographer-facing* tool — the couple's side of the experience is secondary.

## Camrhia

Camrhia starts from a different premise: a wedding isn't one person's project, it's two parties planning one day together. So instead of a photographer-facing tool with a client portal bolted on, the couple gets a genuine, equal seat — they can edit the shared timeline, and either side has to approve changes before they're locked in.

In practice, that means:

- **A timeline both sides actually build together** — not a document one person maintains and the other occasionally checks.
- **Auto-built payment schedules** the moment a wedding is booked, with reminders that send themselves.
- **Contracts that send and sign digitally**, without a separate e-signature tool bolted on.
- **A public profile with real, verified reviews** from actual booked couples — not third-party testimonials.

The tradeoff, in the interest of honesty: Camrhia is newer than both HoneyBook and Dubsado, and doesn't yet have the years of integrations and edge-case workflow customization that Dubsado in particular offers.

## So which one should you pick?

> If you want maximum customization and don't mind investing setup time: **Dubsado**.
> If you want an established, general-purpose tool with a wide feature set: **HoneyBook**.
> If the actual problem you're solving is *keeping a couple genuinely in sync with you* — not just informed — **Camrhia** is built specifically for that.
