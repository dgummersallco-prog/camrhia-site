If your photographer has offered a second shooter as an add-on, it's worth thinking through deliberately rather than defaulting to yes or no based on cost alone.

## When it tends to be genuinely worth it

- **Larger weddings** (150+ guests), where more is happening at once than one photographer can realistically cover
- **You and your partner are getting ready in separate locations**, since one photographer can only be in one place
- **A venue with multiple distinct spaces** happening close together in time — ceremony, cocktail hour, and reception all in different areas
- **You specifically want a lot of candid guest photos**, since a second shooter can work the room independently while the primary photographer focuses on you

## When it's reasonable to skip

- Smaller, more intimate weddings where one photographer can realistically be everywhere that matters
- A tighter budget, where the added cost would mean cutting something else that matters more to you
- A simpler venue layout, where one location covers most of the day

## What to actually ask your photographer

Rather than deciding based on the upsell alone, ask directly: *for our specific guest count and venue, what would we actually gain from a second shooter?* A photographer who can give you a specific answer for your day — not a generic pitch — is giving you real information to decide with.

## It's not an all-or-nothing decision

Some photographers offer a second shooter for just part of the day (getting ready and ceremony, for instance) rather than full coverage, which can be a reasonable middle ground if full second-shooter coverage doesn't fit your budget.
