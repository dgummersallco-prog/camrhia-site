Referral bookings tend to convert far more easily than cold inquiries — someone arrives already trusting your work, because someone they trust vouched for you. Most photographers get referrals passively, when they could be generating them deliberately.

## From past couples

The single highest-leverage referral source is a couple you've already photographed. A few ways to actually activate this instead of just hoping it happens:

- Ask directly, at the right moment — right after gallery delivery, when their excitement about the photos is highest, is a better time to ask for a review or referral than months later.
- Make leaving a review genuinely easy — a two-tap process beats a request to "email us your thoughts."
- Give past couples something specific and shareable, not just "tell your friends."

## From other vendors

Wedding planners, venues, and florists all get asked "do you know a good photographer?" constantly. Building a real relationship with a handful of vendors in your market — not just a one-time introduction — tends to produce steady referral flow over time, since you become their default answer.

## From other photographers

This one's underused: photographers refer clients to each other constantly, particularly for dates they're already booked on, or styles that don't match their own. Being genuinely known and liked within your local photographer community is worth more than most people expect.

## From a structured referral program

Word-of-mouth happens whether you build a system for it or not — but a structured referral incentive (even something informal, like offering value back to whoever refers you) tends to noticeably increase how often people actually follow through on a recommendation, rather than just meaning to.
