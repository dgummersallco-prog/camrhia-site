Of everything you'll book for your wedding, photography carries a strange weight: it's one of the only purchases you genuinely cannot redo. The flowers wilt, the cake gets eaten, but the photos are what's left. That's worth taking seriously when you're choosing who to hire — here's a practical way to do it.

## Start with style, not price

Before comparing costs, look at full wedding galleries (not just highlight reels) from a handful of photographers. You're looking for a *consistent* style across an entire wedding day — not just a few standout shots. Common style categories: bright and airy, moody and dramatic, documentary/candid-focused, or traditional/posed-heavy. Most photographers lean clearly into one.

## Read full galleries, not just portfolios

A curated portfolio shows someone's best 30 photos ever taken. A full gallery from one real wedding shows you what an *entire day* actually looks like in their hands — including the less glamorous moments, like getting-ready shots and reception candids. Ask to see one.

## Ask about their actual process, not just their pricing

The questions that reveal the most:

- What does your day-of timeline planning actually look like? Do we build it together, or do you just show up?
- How do you handle a payment schedule — is it clear from the start what's due and when?
- What's your turnaround time for delivering the final gallery?
- What happens if you're unavailable for some reason on the day — is there a backup plan?

Photographers who answer these clearly and specifically, without vague hand-waving, are usually the ones with genuinely solid systems behind the scenes — and that tends to translate into a smoother experience for you too.

## Watch how they communicate before you've even booked

How a photographer responds to your first inquiry is a real preview of what working with them will feel like for the next several months. Slow, vague, or inconsistent communication before booking rarely improves after you've paid a retainer.

## Book earlier than you think you need to

Photographers in popular markets and peak wedding season often book out 12–18 months in advance. If you've found someone whose work you genuinely love, don't wait for every other vendor to be locked in first.
