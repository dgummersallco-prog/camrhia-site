Most software comparisons focus on feature checklists — contracts: yes, invoicing: yes, scheduling: yes. That's a reasonable starting filter, but it rarely tells you whether a tool will actually fit how you work.

## Start with your actual bottleneck, not a feature list

Before comparing tools, identify what's genuinely costing you the most time or causing the most stress right now — is it chasing payments? Re-typing the same details across multiple places? Couples asking status questions you can't quickly answer? Different tools solve different bottlenecks well; the "best" one depends entirely on which problem is actually yours.

## Consider who else is using it, not just you

If a tool only serves the photographer's side of the relationship — contracts and invoices sent *to* a client, rather than a shared workspace — you're solving half the communication problem. Couples who can only receive information, not meaningfully interact with it, still generate the same status-check messages you were trying to eliminate.

## Weigh setup time honestly

The most powerful, customizable tools often require the most setup time before they save you anything. If you're heading into a busy season, a tool that takes weeks to configure properly may cost you more in the short term than a simpler tool that works well out of the box.

## Check how pricing actually scales

Some tools charge flat regardless of how many active clients you have; others scale with usage. If you expect meaningful growth, model out what the cost looks like at double your current booking volume, not just today's.

## Trial it on a real, active wedding — not a test account

A tool can look complete in a demo and still miss something you only discover mid-use. If a free trial is available, run it against one real, active wedding rather than just clicking through the interface abstractly.
