Waiting for wedding photos is a strange kind of anxious — the wedding itself is over, but this last piece still feels unfinished. Here's a calm, practical way to think through it if your gallery is taking longer than expected.

## First, check what was actually agreed

Before assuming something's wrong, revisit your contract or original conversation about turnaround time. Photographers commonly quote 4-8 weeks, and delivery toward the longer end of that range — especially during peak wedding season, when a photographer may be editing several weddings at once — isn't unusual or necessarily a sign of a problem.

## A reasonable timeline for checking in

If you're still within your photographer's stated turnaround window, there's generally no need to reach out yet. If you've passed it by a week or two with no update, a polite, low-pressure check-in is completely reasonable — most delays have an ordinary explanation and a quick message resolves the anxiety on both sides.

## How to ask, without it feeling like an accusation

Something simple and direct tends to work best: "Hi! Just checking in — do you have a rough estimate on when our gallery might be ready? No rush, just wanted to check." This gives your photographer room to explain without feeling confronted, while still getting you the information you need.

## When it's fair to be more concerned

If your photographer goes significantly past their own stated timeline with no response to reasonable follow-up messages, that's a fair thing to be more concerned about — and worth referencing your contract's terms if the situation continues.

## Most of the time, this resolves itself

The overwhelming majority of "late" galleries are simply a busy photographer working through a genuinely time-intensive process, not a sign anything has gone wrong. A brief, friendly check-in usually settles the anxiety even before the photos themselves arrive.
