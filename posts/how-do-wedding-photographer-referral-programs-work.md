## The short answer

Referral programs in wedding photography generally take one of two forms: informal referral relationships between photographers, vendors, and past clients (often just goodwill or a small thank-you gesture), or formal, structured affiliate programs tied to specific software or services, offering a defined commission for successful referrals.

## Informal referrals

Most wedding-industry referrals happen without any formal program at all — a planner recommends a photographer they trust, a past couple tells a friend, one photographer sends a couple to a colleague who's available on their date. These relationships are typically maintained through genuine goodwill rather than a tracked commission structure.

## Formal, structured affiliate programs

Software and service companies serving the wedding industry increasingly offer structured referral programs, similar to affiliate marketing in other industries. These typically work as follows:

1. A person joins the affiliate program and receives a unique referral link or code
2. They share that link with someone who might use the product or service
3. When that person signs up using the link, the referral is tracked
4. The affiliate earns a commission — either a one-time bounty, or a recurring percentage of what the referred person pays over time

## What varies between programs

- **One-time vs. recurring**: some programs pay a single flat bounty per referral; others pay an ongoing percentage for as long as the referred customer remains a paying subscriber
- **Commission rate**: typically 10-30% for recurring programs, though this varies
- **Duration caps**: many recurring programs cap payouts at a fixed period (often 12 months), while others pay for the full lifetime of the referred subscription
- **Who's eligible**: some programs are open only to existing customers; others are open to anyone

## What to look for in a program before promoting it

Whether a referral program is worth your time generally comes down to three things: is the commission recurring or one-time, is there a cap on how long you're paid, and is the product itself something you'd genuinely recommend regardless of the commission.
